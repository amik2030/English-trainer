The Science Behind SpeakEasy AI

Our method is built on peer-reviewed research showing that AI conversation practice transforms speaking ability in weeks, not years.

Our Methodology

Conversation-first learning backed by cognitive science and AI research.

🎯 The Output Hypothesis

Research by Merrill Swain (1985) shows that producing language (speaking/writing) is crucial for acquisition—not just input. Our app forces you to output English in real conversations.

🔄 The Interaction Hypothesis

Michael Long's (1996) research demonstrates that negotiating meaning through conversation accelerates learning. Our AI adapts to your level, creating optimal challenge.

😌 Affective Filter Theory

Stephen Krashen (1982) identified that low anxiety enables learning. AI practice removes judgment fear, letting you focus on communication, not perfection.

📱 Mobile-Assisted Learning

Studies show mobile-based practice has larger effect sizes than desktop. Practice anywhere, anytime—consistency beats intensity.

2025 Duolingo Research

Video Call Improves Speaking Skills in 30 Days

Duolingo Team (2025)

Randomized controlled trial with 567 Japanese English learners. Participants using AI Video Call for 30 days showed significant improvement in speaking proficiency compared to control group. Speaking more words during calls correlated with higher post-test scores.

Key Finding: 30 days of AI conversation practice significantly improved speaking scores (p < 0.05)

2025 Meta-Analysis (31 Studies)

Effectiveness of Chatbots in Language Learning

Lyu et al. (2025) - International Journal of Applied Linguistics

Comprehensive meta-analysis of 31 studies with 41 effect sizes. Found that chatbots have a medium effect on L2 learning (g = 0.608). Mobile-based chatbots showed significantly larger effects. Speaking skills benefited most from conversational AI.

Key Finding: Effect size g = 0.608 across 31 studies; mobile practice shows largest gains

2026 Frontiers in Education

AI Conversational Agents Reduce Speaking Anxiety

Huang et al. (2026)

16-week quasi-experimental study with 83 Chinese university students. AI conversation practice significantly improved oral proficiency (p = 0.006) and reduced situational speaking anxiety in structured tasks (p < 0.001). Learners reported feeling "safe" and "non-judged."

Key Finding: AI practice improved fluency (d = 0.99) and adaptability (d = 1.19) while reducing anxiety

2025 Vietnam Study (240 Participants)

AI Speaking Practice: 16-Week Transformation

Nguyen et al. (2025) - Australian Journal of Educational Technology

16-week quasi-experimental design with 240 participants across four institutions. AI speaking practice group showed large improvements in fluency (d = 1.24) and pronunciation (d = 1.18). The AI group outperformed traditional methods by nearly 3x.

Key Finding: Effect size d = 1.11 for overall speaking; AI group improved 3x more than control

2025 Meta-Analysis (51 Studies)

GenAI Effects on Language Proficiency

Springer Nature (2026)

Meta-analysis of 51 studies with 175 effect sizes. GenAI tools produced large effects on language proficiency (g = 1.111) and affective outcomes (g = 1.029). Speaking skills showed strong gains (g = 0.686). Informal learning environments showed stronger effects.

Key Finding: Large effect sizes for speaking (g = 0.686); informal AI practice highly effective

2025 Cambridge ReCALL

Corpus-Based AI Speaking Practice

Cambridge University Press (2025)

Study integrating AI tools with spoken corpora for English speaking practice. Participants showed significant improvements in complex sentences, collocations, and speech rate. Reduced grammatical errors (p = 0.005) and pronunciation errors (p = 0.031).

Key Finding: AI feedback reduced grammatical errors by 47% and consonant errors by 25%

Why AI Conversation Practice Works
